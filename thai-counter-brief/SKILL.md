# Thai Food Counter — Immersive Web Experience
**Client:** Thai Street Food Counter
**Project Type:** Website Design & Development
**Skill Plugins:** Antigravity, UI-UX-Pro-Max

---

## The Brief

We're building a website for a Thai street food counter. The vibe should
feel like someone took the raw, electric energy of a Bangkok night market
and filtered it through the lens of a luxury editorial — think *Monocle*
meets *midnight alley neon*.

---

## Design Direction

The design language should feel genuinely alive. Motion should feel
intentional, not decorative — scroll-driven reveals, elements that breathe,
transitions that make the user feel like they're moving *through* a space
rather than down a page. If something can be animated with purpose, animate
it. If it can't, leave it naked and let the typography do the work.

Color, typography, layout — unprescribed. Find the palette that makes the
food feel dangerous and desirable at the same time. Find the typeface that
feels like it was carved into a wooden sign but also belongs on a fashion
week invite. Break the grid where breaking it creates tension. Use negative
space like it costs money.

---

## Section-by-Section Intent

### Hero
Should stop someone mid-scroll. Whether that's a fullscreen immersive scene,
a kinetic typographic statement, or something unexpected — earn the attention.

### Menu
Each dish should feel like it has a personality. Not a list. Not a grid of
cards. A *experience*.

### Reservations / Order Flow
Frictionless and considered. Should feel like a natural continuation of the
design — not a form bolted onto a beautiful page.

---

## Interaction Design

- Micro-interactions that reward curiosity
- Hover states that surprise
- Cursor behavior that feels considered
- Mobile experience as crafted as desktop — not an afterthought
- Sound: optional, but explore it

---

## The Cultural Constraint

It has to feel *Thai* without being a stereotype.

- No lotus flowers as bullet points
- No red-and-gold temple clipart
- No tourist-facing clichés

Find the cultural soul through texture, rhythm, and restraint — or
controlled excess, if that's where the vision goes. The goal is
*authenticity*, not ethnicity as decoration.

---

## Creative Latitude

This brief is directional, not prescriptive. Antigravity and UI-UX-Pro-Max
should explore their full creative depth. The only ask:

> Surprise me. Show me something I couldn't have described myself.

---

## What Success Looks Like

- Feels like it was designed by a MAANG-level design engineer
- Someone screenshots it and sends it to a designer friend
- The food feels alive before anyone reads a word
- Every interaction has been *decided*, not defaulted
